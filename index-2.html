Getting our shit together — for Australia
The situation
You're building skills the future actually needs. Let's use them.
Every day, people across this country are getting deeper into their work — learning how things are designed, engineered, maintained, and governed. Building competency. Developing judgment. Starting to see how systems connect and where they fail.
And yet the big decisions about what Australia builds next — the infrastructure, the systems, the places we'll all live in — are made in closed rooms by people optimising for the next election cycle, not the next fifty years. The people who are deepest in the work, and who'll live longest with the consequences, are barely part of the conversation.
The Ministry of the Future is for anyone who looks at what we've already built — the systems, the knowledge, the culture — and thinks: of course we can do more with this. Of course we can build upon it. Your training, your observations, your ability to imagine and dream and be stubborn about what's possible — that's not just for your employer. That's for all of us.
We're starting with a question: What should Australia build in the next fifty years? And we're using a tool called Agora to find out where we actually agree.
The tool
Agora — how we think together
Agora is an open-source deliberation platform built on the same foundations as Polis — the tool behind Taiwan's vTaiwan process, where citizens shaped actual legislation through structured online deliberation. It maps the opinion landscape of any group, reveals hidden consensus, and structures conversation through four phases.
01
✦
Dream
Wide-open brainstorm. Everyone submits ideas and votes agree, disagree, or pass. No constraints, no filtering — just the raw material of collective imagination.
02
◈
Shape
The opinion map is revealed. Clusters emerge. Participants see where they sit in the landscape and respond with more nuanced, bridging contributions. The picture gets clearer.
03
◆
Commit
Consensus items become action items. Participants signal what they'll help make happen. Ideas become plans. Plans find people.
04
○
Reflect
What worked? What surprised us? The conversation becomes a record and a reference — seeding the next iteration of collective thinking.
Under the hood
Faithful implementation of proven methods
Agora implements the core algorithms described in the Polis research paper, adapted for phased deliberation and community-scale deployment.
Dimensionality Reduction
PCA via Power Iteration
Votes form a matrix. Principal component analysis projects participants into a 2D opinion space — a map of where people stand, computed from how they actually voted.
Clustering
K-Means with Silhouette Selection
Opinion groups emerge automatically. The algorithm tests multiple cluster counts and selects the one that best captures the real structure of disagreement.
Consensus Detection
Group-Aware Consensus Metric
Consensus isn't just majority agreement. Agora computes the product of agreement across all groups — protecting minority dissent and surfacing genuine cross-group common ground.
Bridging
Cross-Cluster Agreement Scoring
The most valuable statements are those that unite otherwise-separated groups. Bridging scores identify the ideas with the power to transcend tribal boundaries.
Missing Data
Sparsity-Aware Correction
Not everyone votes on every statement. The √(C/Cp) scaling factor corrects for this, preventing under-voters from clumping at the centre of the map.
Comment Routing
Priority-Weighted Distribution
Statements are shown in a semi-random order weighted toward those most likely to build consensus, have high PCA loading, or are new and need exposure.

What we are
Ministry of the Future
Engineers Designers Builders Observers Stubborn Optimists
You're years into building a competency. You're learning how systems work — how structures carry load, how water moves through landscapes, how communities use space, how supply chains hold or break, how design shapes behaviour. That knowledge isn't just for your career. It's for all of us.
The Ministry of the Future is a growing network of people — especially young engineers, designers, and professionals — who believe that the skills they're building day to day should help inform what Australia actually builds next. Not in fifty years when you're senior enough to be in the room. Now. While you still have the energy, the stubbornness, and the imagination to insist on something better.
But this isn't only for professionals. The parent who knows which intersection is dangerous, the farmer who knows where the water actually flows, the teenager who knows why nobody catches the bus — contextual awareness is expertise too. Everyone who pays attention to the world around them has something this conversation needs.
We acknowledge the incredible agency and power in our abilities and culture right now. We also acknowledge the disconnect. So we're finding new ways to bring people together — for the joy of living, dreaming, playing and working together toward something worth building. Because if the future doesn't look better than the present, we're going to be angry and disappointed with ourselves.
This isn't anyone's personal project. It belongs to everyone who shows up. The platform is open source. The conversations are open. Of course we can build upon what the systems of our world have already provided us. Of course we will.
We will all die in the not too distant future. What we build together in the meantime is what matters.
